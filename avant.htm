What made Tiemko
Toussaint, then a bass player, and Delaunay, met in 1976 as members of a band named Phénix. They spent whole afternoons devoted to listening to ia King Crimson, Genesis, Zappa, Gentle Giant, Art Zoyd, Deep Purple, Queen or Shostakovich, Bartok, Dutilleux, Messiaen, Stravinsky... They recorded their own compositions for piano, vibraphone, drums on Revox, also cassette, in a vast duplex apartment that had been used as a workshop for Sonia Delaunay, Éric's grandmother, in Paris 7ème, rue Saint-Simon. Desdemona, Doctor Mad, had a first studio experience with singer Bevinda – who introduced him to literary and theater man †Gilles Canuto, with whom a great complicity was established as regards literature, sport, theater or staging, raving about on impossible texts, writing improbable songs – then Marie Grenon (see Marie Faye), whom he accompanied in concert with Jean-François "Framboise" Bouvier. The keyboardist composer and arranger François Bréant stired Rémy's curiosity for the universes of Zappa, Varese, Charles Yves, Messiaen... In parallel to Tiemko, Rémy took lessons in a school of jazz while working on soundtracks, for cinema, documentaries or animation...
