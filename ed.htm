ÉRIC DELAUNAY 1953-1996
Before Tiemko he produced "ANTAGONISME !" (1981) and was behind DEBOCO. Jean-Jacques shares some memories of their times together:
Éric in his grandma's workshop
Before forming TIEMKO, we had decided with Eric to work on mutual compositions. We met regularly in the apartment he then occupied in Paris, rue Saint-Simon in the 7th arrondissement. Armed with a piano, a vibraphone and drums, we put together a number of tracks which we then recorded on a revox (I still have some of these pieces on tape). The experiment lasted for around a year... This apartment (a vast duplex) had been the studio of his grandmother Sonia Delaunay, whom I had had the opportunity to meet a few years earlier in the company of Eric. There were all sorts of canvases made up everywhere... Most of them by Sonia Delaunay, but without a signature because they were most often preparatory works. I remember a canvas, a kind of big yellow spot which burst like a star, that stumbled on the piano and bothered me. Eric told me "You can fire that lousy shit"; "And whose lousy shit is this?" I asked him. "From Max Ernst"... he replied. Eric was living on incomes from family assets.
Jean-Jacques on Eric's lyrics for Tiemko
There is a large part of autobiography, discomfort (within relationships but also related to his childhood, and then his adult life - where he feels outside of traditional society), of feelings (Eric was very intuitive: he saw things right even if without any nuances), a more than pessimistic vision of what surrounds him; Eric doesn't cheat.
Apocalypse is a great achievement, an extraordinary song; the lyrics and the music are in perfect harmony on a background of dissonance (dissonance and uselessness of existence which contrasts with a martial beat: Eric was fascinated by war machines; some of his paintings have just tanks on them. Faut que je me demerde pour des pots cassés que je n'ai pas cassé et rempli de merde I must sort my shit out for broken pots that I didn't break – and are full of shit (awkard childhood then existence) Dans le monde qu'on vit et qui pue la gerbe, qu'est-ce qu'on peut bien foutre les pieds dans la merde In the world we live in, that smells of puke, what the hell can we do with our feet in shit (uselessness of existence) Et que le bateau n'est pas à bon port And that the boat is not safe (we're not going anywhere) – here my voice is along Eric's – At the end, the guitar finds itself alone, without effect, with no way out... It scatters without any goal.
