TIEMKO en ligne
JEAN-JACQUES TOUSSAINT 1956
Sur fond d'écoute de Travelling et Droit de rêve: A few words by the polymath ia composer, archaeologist, teacher Jean-Jacques...
... Jean-Jacques describes Tiemko
What's told about Tiemko on Internet is sometimes stereotyped and resembles too often to copied quotes, like it happens too often on the Internet. One would read "utilisation très particulière des dissonances" (Are there any unusual dissonances ?),"led by a cataclysmic guitar" (I didn't know Rémy had affinities with the Apocalypse) and "unsettling keyboards" (sometimes they talked about dark keyboards — I hate the dark), (...)this all seems a bit simplistic to me - to be polite - even if simplier terms sometimes seem more accurate to me: originality, rhythmic inventiveness, personal and experimental concept, singular and inspired music, iconoclasm... I don't know if [any] interview mentions sung titles (from Clone) that I would very much like to rehabilitate. Diehard makers of dark and cataclysmic music would allegedly only produce instrumental titles; "Do you want to play commercial music ?" is what Musea told me on the phone, they never defended (it's even the opposite) neither the originality of the music nor the lyrics of the songs there (which as everyone knows were all first in the charts...).
... sur Phoenix
Long before Tiemko, guitarist Jean Marc Thiebaut was part of a first band (Phoenix) in which I was bass player; there were two others guitarists (Philippe Bambuck and Vévé – because of this diminutive, I completely forgot his name –), Robert Kempler on keyboard (who will play with Asia Minor) and of course Eric to the battery.
... about Jean-Michel Brière
Eric, then all of us, became friends with Jean-Michel Brière. A talented and original character to whom we owe, among other things, the the cover of Clone (supervised by Eric). It was he who allowed us to be invited for a private concert, a big party (birthday) the filmmaker for whom he was working at the time [Alain Tyr]; the latter in exchange made Tiemko's only video; not so unique as that, because there were many in concert, all of which have disappeared: but that's another story...
... sur Gilles Canuto
Gilles Canuto (...) je l'ai bien connu (...) j'ai travaillé avec lui pour mettre en musique ses textes. C'est ou c'était, hélas, un personnage hors normes que j'aimais beaucoup. J'ai repris un de ces textes (encore une chanson qui fit faire la grimace à Muséa) sur Clone : Rock and Roll Alice.
... sur Pip Pyle
Pip Pyle, ce superbe batteur (...), arrive après Tiemko grâce à Rémy. Il devait faire les batteries sur mon album "Travelling" ; on a commencé à travailler ensemble puis un mois avant d'entrer au studio, il a cassé sa Pip, si je peux me permettre ce jeux de mots désastreux. Car sa disparition a été désastreuse à tout point de vue.
